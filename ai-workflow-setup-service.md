A concrete, beginner-accessible path to building and selling AI workflow automation services to small businesses. The skill can be learned over a weekend; the first client can be approached by Monday.

## The Opportunity

~76% of small businesses are actively seeking AI integration but don't know where to start or who to trust. ~90% of SMBs have implemented no meaningful AI automation. This gap is the business.

The service: audit a small business's most repetitive manual tasks, identify the top 2–3 time-wasters, and build simple AI-powered workflows that eliminate them. Entry price: **$500 per setup**. Monthly retainers of $500–$5,000 are common once established.

This is not a race to the bottom on Fiverr. You are having a direct conversation with a specific business owner about their specific pain — almost no competition, high perceived value.

## The Weekend Learning Plan

### Saturday Morning: Learn the Landscape (3 hours)
Three core tools:
- **Make.com** or **Zapier** — automation platforms that connect apps (triggers → actions)
- **Claude or ChatGPT** — the intelligence layer that transforms unstructured input (e.g. a customer email) into structured output (e.g. a categorised support ticket or draft reply)

Goal: understand what a "scenario" and "module" are in Make.com, and how data flows between steps. Use Make.com's official YouTube tutorials.

### Saturday Afternoon: Build Something Real
Don't practice on fake data. Build a workflow for yourself — email logging, newsletter summarisation, social media monitoring. You'll break it, fix it, break it again. That process is the actual learning.

### Sunday: Learn the Sales Conversation
Study the most common manual tasks in small businesses:
- Email triage and response drafting
- Lead capture and CRM entry
- Appointment reminders and follow-ups
- Invoice generation
- Social media scheduling
- Internal reporting

For each, practice answering: *What does this cost the business in time and money per month, and what does solving it look like?*

Example: a business with two employees spending 3 hrs/week on manual data entry is paying ~$300/month in labour on a problem a $500 automation solves permanently.

## Monday: The Offer and Outreach

**The offer:** a free one-hour AI workflow audit where you identify the most time-consuming manual tasks and show exactly how automation would solve them. If they want you to build it: $500.

**Who to contact:** 5 people you already know who own or manage small businesses. Not cold outreach.

**Message template:**
> "Hey, I've been learning AI workflow automation and I'm doing a few free audits this week to practise. You'd get a clear picture of which tasks could be automated and what the impact would be. No pitch, no pressure. Want to do a quick call?"

**On the call — three questions:**
1. What tasks does your team do repeatedly that feel like they should be automatic?
2. Where do things fall through the cracks because of manual handoffs?
3. What would you do with five extra hours per week?

Listen more than you talk. By the end, you'll know exactly what to build.

## What a $500 Delivery Looks Like

One to three connected automations solving a defined, recurring problem. Example:

- Lead fills out a form →
- Record created in CRM +
- Personalised email drafted by Claude and sent to lead +
- Slack notification to business owner summarising the inquiry

This eliminates ~2 hours/week of manual work for a business with 10 leads/week, and eliminates the risk of leads falling through the cracks.

## After Monday

If 1–2 of 5 audits convert: $500–$1,000 from a weekend's learning.

**Natural progression:**
1. **Retainer** — once you've built an automation, clients want it maintained and expanded. $500+/month.
2. **Productising** — after building the same solution 3 times, package it at a fixed price with minimal customisation. Dental practices all need appointment reminders; real estate agents all need lead follow-up.
