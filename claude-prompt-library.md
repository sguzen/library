Two complementary prompt collections: 25 practical prompts for weekly productivity (writing, research, analysis, coding, productivity) and 10 deep strategy prompt systems for life architecture, career, and decision-making. Both follow the same principle: replace vague instructions with structured templates that constrain the AI into producing specific, non-generic output.

## The 25 Practical Prompts (15 Hours Saved Per Week)

### Writing & Content

**01. The First Draft Killer**
> I need a first draft of [TYPE] about [TOPIC]. Audience: [WHO]. Tone: [TONE]. Length: [COUNT]. Does NOT sound like: generic AI, corporate jargon, LinkedIn influencer. Does NOT include: filler phrases, unnecessary caveats, "in today's fast-paced world". Here is an example of my writing style: [PASTE EXAMPLE]. Match the voice and energy. Write a complete first draft.

*Why it works: negative constraints kill the AI smell; the voice example teaches Claude better than any description.*

**02. The Thread Expander**
> Turn this idea into a 10-post X/Twitter thread: [IDEA]. Rules: Post 1 = curiosity hook or bold claim. Each post = 1–3 sentences max. Specific examples and numbers only. Post 10 = clear CTA. No hashtags. No emojis unless necessary. Does NOT sound like a motivational speaker or LinkedIn guru.

**03. The Content Repurposer**
> I have one piece of long-form content. Repurpose it into: 5 standalone tweets (each works independently), 2 LinkedIn posts (professional but not boring, max 200 words each), 3 Instagram captions (casual, under 150 words), 1 email teaser (under 100 words, curiosity-driven subject line). Keep voice consistent. Adapt length and tone to each platform. [PASTE CONTENT]

**04. The Headline Machine**
> Generate 20 headline variations for [TOPIC] using: curiosity gap, specific numbers, how-to, contrarian, social proof, "I want to" formats (at least 2 per framework). Rank your top 5 and explain why each would stop someone mid-scroll.

**05. The Email Sequence Writer**
> Write a 5-email welcome sequence for [PRODUCT/SERVICE]. Target: [WHO]. Pain point: [PROBLEM]. Goal by email 5: [ACTION]. For each email: subject line, preview text, body (under 200 words), send timing, A/B subject variation. Email 1 delivers value, no selling. Email 2 tells a story. Email 3 introduces solution subtly. Email 4 = social proof. Email 5 = direct ask with urgency.

**06. The SEO Content Brief**
> Create a complete brief for [KEYWORD]: title, meta description, URL slug, word count, H2/H3 outline, 5 related keywords, 3 internal linking opportunities, 2 external source suggestions, featured snippet opportunity. Detailed enough that any writer could produce the article without additional research.

**07. The Voice Cloner**
> Analyze these writing samples and extract my voice: [PASTE 2–3 PIECES]. Create a Voice Profile: sentence length patterns, vocabulary level, 5 tone adjectives, structural habits, frequent phrases, things I never do, energy level. Then write a 200-word sample paragraph about [TOPIC] using this exact profile.

*Save the Voice Profile output as a context file. Every future prompt gets your voice right on the first pass.*

### Research & Analysis

**08. The Meeting Prep Brief**
> I have a meeting with [PERSON] from [COMPANY] in [TIME]. Research and create a one-page brief: background, company overview, recent news, 3 relevant talking points on [TOPIC], 3 smart questions to ask, 1 common ground to build rapport. Format for 5-minute reading before the call.

**09. The Decision Matrix**
> I need to decide between [OPTIONS]. My priorities (ranked): [1, 2, 3]. For each option: score 1–10 on each priority, top 2 risks, top 2 advantages, what would need to be true for it to be best. Then give me your recommended option in 3 sentences. Do NOT hedge. Pick one and defend it.

**10. The Competitor Breakdown**
> Analyze [COMPETITOR] as if you were their CMO preparing a competitive brief for our team. Cover: positioning, target audience, key messages, pricing strategy, content strategy, top 3 weaknesses we can exploit, top 3 strengths we need to respect.

**11. The Research Synthesizer**
> I've been researching [TOPIC] for [TIME]. Here's everything I've collected: [PASTE NOTES/LINKS/EXCERPTS]. Synthesize this into: 1) the 3 most important insights, 2) what the sources agree on, 3) where they contradict each other and why, 4) the single most useful thing I should do with this information. Flag anything I might be missing.

**12. The Devil's Advocate**
> I've decided to [DECISION]. I'm confident. Play devil's advocate. Don't be gentle. Find the strongest possible case against this decision. Assume I've missed something important. Give me the 3 most compelling reasons I might be wrong, with specific evidence or reasoning for each.

**13. The Trend Report**
> Summarize the current state of [INDUSTRY/TOPIC] in 2026. Cover: where it was 2 years ago, what changed and why, what the current consensus believes, what the contrarian view is, where it's heading in the next 12–18 months, and what someone building in this space should know that most people don't yet.

**14. The Literature Review**
> I need to understand [ACADEMIC TOPIC] quickly. Give me: 3 foundational papers everyone cites, 3 recent papers that changed the conversation, the main debates in the field, where current research is focused, and the most useful mental model for a non-expert to use.

### Personal Productivity

**15. The Weekly Review Prompt**
> Run my weekly review. I'll tell you what happened this week: [PASTE]. Help me identify: what I should keep doing, what I should stop doing, what I should start doing, and the single most important thing to focus on next week. Be specific. Don't let me off easy.

**16. The Energy Audit**
> I'm going to describe my last week in detail: [DESCRIBE]. Identify: which activities gave me energy and which drained it, any patterns you notice, what this suggests about how I should structure my time, and 2 specific changes I could make this week to have more energy by Friday.

**17. The Procrastination Diagnosis**
> I keep avoiding [TASK]. I've been avoiding it for [TIME]. Help me figure out why. Ask me questions one at a time until you've diagnosed the real reason. Then give me the smallest possible first step that would make the task feel manageable.

## The 10 Deep Strategy Prompt Systems

These are full conversation architectures, not single prompts. Each is a multi-phase session designed for a specific life domain.

**System 1: The Complete Life Audit**
A full-system look across 9 domains (career, money, health, mind, relationships, romance, environment, purpose, growth). Starts with psychological excavation (root patterns, identity traps, anti-vision), runs through each domain, and ends with a concrete implementation plan including life architecture, vision, daily levers, and weekly reset. Designed to take 2–4 hours of honest engagement.

*Trigger phrase:* "Run my life audit" — then describe your current situation honestly.

**System 2: Career Reinvention**
Maps your current skills and experience to 3–5 non-obvious career pivots. Evaluates each against your actual priorities (not assumed ones). Produces a 90-day concrete transition plan for the chosen direction.

**System 3: Money Architecture**
Full personal finance audit → income ceiling analysis → specific wealth-building levers → implementation plan. Designed to surface the gap between where money actually goes and where it should go.

**System 4: Health System Design**
Builds a minimum-viable health protocol based on your actual constraints (time, preferences, history). Not a generic diet plan — a system calibrated to what you'll actually maintain.

**System 5: Relationship Audit**
Maps every significant relationship against your actual needs. Identifies who drains, who energizes, who you're underinvesting in, and what one conversation would change the most.

**System 6: Focus Architecture**
Diagnoses the specific ways your attention gets fragmented and builds a daily structure that protects deep work. Produces rules specific to your situation, not generic advice.

**System 7: Identity Engineering**
Identifies the self-concepts running your behavior (the "I am the type of person who..." statements). Distinguishes which serve you and which are cages. Rebuilds identity around the person you're trying to become.

**System 8: Decision Operating System**
Documents your actual decision-making style, identifies recurring failure patterns, and builds a personal decision framework calibrated to your known weaknesses.

**System 9: Environment Design**
Audits home, work, social, and digital environments for friction (things that make bad habits easy) and resistance (things that make good habits hard). Produces a room-by-room, app-by-app redesign.

**System 10: Purpose Excavation**
A deep-dive conversation that surfaces what actually matters to you — distinct from what you think should matter. Produces a purpose statement you can test against real decisions.

*For all systems: paste the relevant prompt at the start of a fresh Claude session. Give real answers. Push back on any response that feels generic.*
