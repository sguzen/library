Most people who learn vibe coding (building software with AI coding assistants) never make money from it because they build demos without targeting real demand. This guide maps six income paths with specific implementation steps, ranked from lowest to highest complexity.

## Why People Fail

The problem isn't skill level. It's building without a target customer. Todo apps and portfolio clones don't generate income; solutions to documented, recurring pain points do.

## The Six Income Paths

### 1. Small Business Websites — Super Easy

**Why it works:** Millions of small businesses have no website or a terrible one. Before AI, building sites was expensive. Now it costs a few dollars in tokens per site.

**Customer acquisition system:**
1. Set up a crawler (Firecrawl or similar) in your terminal
2. Fetch emails and social media contacts of businesses in a target niche/city
3. Use an email service for bulk outreach
4. Use a social media agent to automate cold DMs
5. Serve interested parties and deliver

Pricing varies by market. For higher-income cities, charge proportionally more. Volume is the model at this level.

### 2. Custom Tools for Small Businesses — Easy

**Why it works:** Small businesses need software but can't afford $10K+ dev shops. Appointment systems, inventory trackers, lead capture forms, basic dashboards. Build time: 1–2 days. Charge setup fee + $50–$100/month maintenance.

**Implementation:**
1. Use the same acquisition process as websites
2. Send a form asking prospects to describe exactly what they need
3. Confirm their exact user journey and outcomes before building
4. Build with AI coding assistant (most take 1–2 days)
5. Deploy, walk them through it, set up monthly maintenance agreement

**Niche play:** Go vertical. Walk into hair salons, gyms, restaurants — ask what they hate doing manually. Build one solution for many businesses in the same niche. Build once, customize lightly, sell many times.

### 3. Templates, Starter Kits, and Prompt Packs — Easy

**Why it works:** Digital products have near-zero marginal cost. Build once, sell repeatedly.

**What sells:**
- SaaS boilerplates with auth and billing already configured
- Notion templates for specific workflows
- Prompt libraries for consistent AI output in specific niches
- Figma kits

**Distribution:** Gumroad, Lemon Squeezy, or Stripe-connected landing page. Post in communities where buyers already are (subreddits, Discord servers, X). Let the first 10 buyers tell you what to improve.

**Key insight:** The best digital products come from your own frustrations. Package your problem and solution — others with the same problem will find you.

### 4. Paid Workflow Automations — Medium

**Why it works:** "Annoyance work" tied to revenue and operations (spreadsheet cleaning, report generation, document formatting, lead compilation) is a goldmine. Buyers move fast and churn is low.

**Finding clients:**
1. Search X, Reddit, LinkedIn for "I spend 3 hours every week on..." — that's your signal
2. DM 5–10 people, ask about their workflow, don't pitch yet
3. Build a demo for the simplest problem; record a 2-minute video showing it with their use case
4. Send the video: "I built this for you." Iterate until perfect
5. Charge setup fee + monthly maintenance

**Stack:** n8n, Make, Zapier. One automation built for one client can usually be sold to 5–10 others with minor customization.

### 5. SaaS Products — Hard

**Why it works:** Recurring revenue with equity value. AI lets solo builders ship what previously required teams.

**The pattern that works:** Find a niche where:
- The existing solution is enterprise-priced or too complex for SMBs
- The core functionality can be simplified into 3–5 screens
- You can get 10 paying customers before building more than the MVP

AI coding assistants reduce solo SaaS build time from months to weeks, but this still requires sustained effort and distribution strategy.

### 6. Consulting and Agency Work — Expert

**Why it works:** Your track record of building 1–5 projects becomes proof that you can solve business problems with AI. Charge for your judgment, not your hours.

**Transition path:** Complete paths 1–4, collect 2–3 strong case studies, then position as an AI implementation consultant. This connects to the income stack described in [[claude-skills-income-stack]].

## The Compounding Pattern

Each path reinforces the others:
- Websites and tools generate testimonials and referrals
- Templates prove expertise and generate inbound
- Automations become SaaS features
- All of them become case studies for consulting

The people who fail do one path and quit when it doesn't immediately work. The people who succeed run multiple paths simultaneously and let them feed each other.
